# Brain-like-models

INTRODUCTION: 
Ever since the dawn of artificial intelligence. What is it that actually makes a model brain-like? 

This question gives rise to another question: Does a vision model's task performance predict how brain-like its internal representations are or is it the training objective, the architecture or sheer scale? This is something that no one knows the answer to yet.

Better models are more brain-like related to Platonic Representation Hypothesis, this correlation however, keeps running into trouble. Recent work finds the correlation decouples or weakens past a point, that intermediate layers tend to align better than final layers, and that language-aligned and self-supervised models are often surprisingly brain-like for their accuracy. That instability is exactly why this generates more questions than it answers.
